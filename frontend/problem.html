The Mission

Problem Statement

Engineering drawings have become more complex, but the tools we use to clean them haven't kept up. DraftClear was built to solve the systematic issues that plague CAD-to-PDF workflows.

The Challenge

  • Text Overlapping: Dense engineering drawings often result in unreadable, stacked labels that require manual correction.
  • SHX Font Ghosting: Legacy CAD fonts often create "phantom" text comments in PDF exports, creating messy artifacts.
  • Duplicate Entities: Stacking of lines and text objects increases file size and confuses AI perception engines.
  • Manual Iteration: Engineers waste up to 30% of their drawing time simply "making things look clean."

The DraftClear Solution

  • Autonomous Resolution: AI agents calculate optimal, collision-free placements in real-time using LangGraph.
  • Deep Pipeline Integration: Automatic `PDFSHX` and `OVERKILL` logic solves ghosting at the source.
  • Intelligent Thresholding: Otsu's algorithm allows the AI to see through colored boxes and complex gradients.
  • Deduplication engine: "AI Overkill" filters out phantom objects before they ever reach the final report.

Technical Dictionary

Understanding the AI's Mission

Text Labels

In CAD drawing terms, Text Labels are the intelligence of the document. They include measurements (e.g., "10mm"), equipment IDs ("Valve-01"), or descriptions. Without these labels, a technical drawing is just a set of lines without meaning. DraftClear treats these labels as the primary objects of interest, ensuring they remain readable and correctly associated with their geometry.

Collisions

A Collision occurs when a Text Label overlaps with geometry (lines, shapes) or another label, making it unreadable. These are often caused by dense engineering layouts or automatic placement logic. DraftClear's AI identifies these incidents, "heals" the background geometry, and repositions the label in a mathematically calculated Safe Zone to ensure 100% legibility.
